Traditional Recipes

Here are some delicious tradtional Māori recipes that you can make yourself.

Gordon Ramsey, eat your heart out.

Creamed paua-soup

Delve into the flavours of the sea with this delightful Paua soup, packed with rich seafood goodness.

(4 Servings).

Ingredients:

  • 50g butter
  • 2c roughly chopped onion
  • zest of 1 lemon
  • pinch of dried chilli flakes
  • 1 1/2T chopped fresh tarragon
  • 5 paua (800g), beaten and roughly chopped
  • 1 1/2 litres chicken or fish stock
  • 1c cream
  • Squeeze of lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper

Directions:

Start by cooking the onion, celery, garlic, lemon zest, chili, and tarragon in a pan on low heat. Make sure not to cook them too much, about half an hour is good to bring out all the flavours without browning them too much.


After that, add the paua and stock to the pan. Bring it to a boil, then let it simmer for at least an hour. If the liquid reduces too much, you can add some water.


Paua doesn't get super soft like slow-cooked meat, so after about 1 1/2 - 2 hours of cooking, take it off the heat. Add the cream, and use a food processor or blender to blend everything until it's smooth. Blend it really well to get rid of the chunky texture.


Finally, taste the soup and add salt, pepper, and lemon to make it just how you like it.

Rewana Bread

Māori bread, known as "Rewana" bread, is a cherished cultural symbol in New Zealand.

Rich in heritage, it embodies a unique tangy flavour derived from its potato-based starter culture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of diced potato
  • 2 tsp of sugar
  • 4 1/2 cups of plain flour (main)
  • 1 cup of wholemeal flour
  • 3 tsp of salt

Directions:

Boil potatoes in 2 cups of water, then let them cool without adding salt.


Add sugar and half a cup of flour, mash it all together to make a batter.


Put the batter in a large jar and cover it with a clean cloth. Leave it in a warm place for 24 hours until it ferments and doubles in size, showing bubbles.


Finally, taste the soup and add salt, pepper, and lemon to make it just how you like it.


When the batter's at its peak, start making bread dough.


For the bread, mix the remaining plain and wholemeal flour with the potato batter, 200ml water, and salt. Knead it by hand or with a machine for about 8 minutes until it's smooth.


Put the bread dough in an oiled bowl, cover it, and let it rise in a warm spot until it doubles in size (about 4 hours).


Preheat the oven to 230°C. Place the dough on a baking tray, sprinkle with flour, make slits on top, and add two ice cubes on the tray to help it rise.


Bake for 35 minutes, then lower the temperature to 210°C for another 10 minutes until the crust is dark golden brown and tapping the base sounds hollow.
