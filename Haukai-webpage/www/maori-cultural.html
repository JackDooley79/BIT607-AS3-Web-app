-History-

Around 1000 years ago, the skilled Polynesian navigator Kupe discovered New Zealand, sailing from his homeland Hawaiki. He bestowed upon these lands the name Aotearoa, translating to "Land of the Long White Cloud." Upon returning to Hawaiki, Kupe left behind instructions guiding others to this newfound place. Approximately 400 years later, the Māori people voyaged back to Aotearoa in seven impressive canoes, initiating the settlement and population of the North Island.

In 1642, Dutch explorer Abel Tasman made landfall, mapping sections of the coastline and initially naming the territory Staten Land, presuming it to be part of the Australian continent. Upon realizing his error, the region was renamed Nieuw Zeeland. James Cook arrived in 1769, extensively mapping both the North and South Islands and renaming the Bay of Islands to its current designation during his exploration of New Zealand.

Māori arrival to New Zealand

The Bay of Islands has witnessed a range of Māori iwi (tribes) settling across the centuries. The initial tribes colonized the region in the 10th century but shortly moved out of the bay, their remnants are seen on Moturua Island at Urimatao where archeologists found garden sites. The Te Awa people from Dargaville followed, leaving their mark at Rawhiti and Manawaora with fortified villages.

In the 15th century, the Ngare Raumati arrived, blending with local sub-tribes for the next three hundred years. The 17th century saw conflicts and land exchanges, notably the Kororāreka Peninsula changing hands as utu (revenge) between the Ngā Manu and Waipihangarangi descendants.

By the early 19th century, Ngāpuhi chiefs dominated the region, prompting the departure of Ngare Raumati (later known as Ngāti Manu) inland. This era marked Kororareka's transition, influenced significantly by interactions with European newcomers, altering its landscape forever.

-Useful Māori Words-

Have a go at learning these useful Māori words.

Kia Ora

This means be healthy but is a common greeting.

pō mārie

Goodnight.

Kai

This means food.

Ka pai

Well done or good.

Inu

This means to drink.
